Part 1: The Gravitational Potential of the Earth¶

The gravitational potential at a distance $r$ from a mass $M$ is given by:

$$\Phi(r) = -\frac{GM}{r}$$

where G is the gravitational constant.

def pot(xm,ym,x,y,M, rad):
    """
    Retuns the gravitational potential of a mass at (x,y) 'p' due to mass M at (xm,ym).
    Arguments :(xm,ym,x,y,M, rad)
    All arguments must be either floats or integers.
    - The function cannot compute the potential at the same location of the mass M
    
    Returns p, the gravitational potential.
    """
    if not isinstance(M,float) and not isinstance(M,int):
        raise TypeError('The variable \'M\' needs to be a float or an integer')
    r = np.hypot((xm-x),(ym-y))
    if np.isclose(r,rad):
        raise ValueError('The potential cannot be computed at the location of the point mass \'M\'')
        return np.nan
    p = -G*M/r
    return p

Part 3: The Gravitational Force Field of the Earth-Moon System¶

The gravitational force $\vec{F}$ that a mass $M_1$ exerts on a mass $m_2$ is:

$$\vec{F}_{21} = -G \frac{M_1 m_2}{|\vec{r}_{21}|^2} \hat{r}_{21}$$

where $\vec{r}_{21}$ is the displacement vector from $M_1$ to $m_2$.

Note that the gravitational force contains a singularity, and so if you made the approximation that the Earth is a finite point, you will need to avoid it. def F(M,m,x1,y1,x2,y2, rad):
    """
    Returns the gravitational force between two masses M and m.
    Arguments:
    M (mass of the first object)
    m (mass of the second object)
    x1 (x coordinate of the first object)
    y1 (y coordinate of the first object)
    x2 (X coordinate of the second object)
    y2 (Y coordinate of the second object)
    x (A x coordinate)
    y (A y coordinate)

    - The function cannot compute the gravitational force where (x1,y1) = (x2,y2)

    Returns the x and y component of gravitational force at x2 and y2
    """
    r = np.hypot((x2-x1),(y2-y1))
    if not isinstance(M,float) and not isinstance(M,int):
        raise TypeError('The variable \'M\' needs to be a float or an integer')
    if not isinstance(m,float) and not isinstance(m,int):
        raise TypeError('The variable \'m\' needs to be a float or an integer')
    if np.isclose(r,rad):
        return np.nan, np.nan
    else:
        F = -1*G*M*m/r**2
        dx = (x2-x1)
        dy = (y2-y1)
        rx = dx/r
        ry = dy/r
        Fx = F*rx
        Fy = F*ry
        return Fx, Fy

Part 4: Altitude of the Saturn V Rocket¶

Rockets function by conservation of momentum. The ejection of fuel backwards propels the rocket forwards. The change in a rocket's velocity $\Delta v$ as a function of time $t$ (under a set of simplifying assumptions) is given by the Tsiolkovsky rocket equation:

$$\Delta v(t) = v_e \ln \left(\frac{m_0}{m(t)}\right) - gt$$

where $m_0$ is the initial "wet" mass (fuel + rocket parts + payload), $m(t) = m_0 - \dot{m}t$ is the mass at time $t$, $\dot{m}$ is the fuel burn rate (which we will assume to be constant), $v_e$ is the fuel exhaust velocity, and $g$ is the gravitational acceleration.

You can find the altitude $h$ of the rocket at "burnout" (when all the fuel is used up) by integrating the velocity over time:

$$h = \int_{0}^{T} \Delta v(t) \, dt$$

where $T$ is the total burn time of the rocket, which by conservation of momentum arguments is:

$$T = \frac{m_0 - m_f}{\dot{m}}$$

where $m_f$ is the final "dry mass" of the rocket, once all the fuel is burned.

def v(t,mw= m0 ,md = mf,br =mr,es = ve,gr = g):
    """
    Returns the change in velocity of the saturn v rocket at a given time 't'.
    Arguments:
    t : Time for which the rocket has been burning
    mw : Initial mass of the rocket
    md: Final mass of the rocket 
    br: Burn rate of the mass
    es: Exhaust speed of the rocket
    gr: Gravitational acceleration due to the earth

    - The function considers no chance in velocity after all fuel has been exhausted.
    """
    if t <= ((mw-md)/br):
        v = es*np.log(mw/(m0-t*br))-g*t

    else:
        v = np.nan
    #v = np.where(t <= ((mw-md)/br), es*np.log(mw/(m0-t*br))-g*t, np.nan)
    return v
